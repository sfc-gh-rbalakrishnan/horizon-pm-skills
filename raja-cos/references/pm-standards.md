## Customer Engagement Standards

### Meeting Cadence
- **Minimum:** ≥ 4 external customer meetings per PM per week
- **What counts:** Any meeting where a customer is present and product topics are discussed (discovery, check-in, EBC, QBR, escalation, advisory call)
- **What does NOT count:** Internal Snowflake syncs, partner briefings without customer, recruiting calls

### Why This Matters
PMs who fall below 4 customer meetings/week for 2+ consecutive weeks are losing market signal. This is the single strongest leading indicator of roadmap drift. The manager's job is to keep the cadence, not just measure it.

## Meeting Notes Standards

### Required Fields per Note
- Customer name and date
- Attendees (Snowflake + customer side)
- Top 3 things the customer said (verbatim or close paraphrase — not paraphrased into PM-speak)
- Open requests or asks (named, specific)
- Product signals: features mentioned, gaps surfaced, competitors mentioned
- Hypothesis validation: which hypothesis was tested, what was learned
- Action items with owner and due date

### Filing Standard
- All notes filed in the centralized Google Drive folder within **48 hours** of the meeting
- Folder: https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1_0K6mAf1EeCuOhixDhABGOIOSEbf8JTu
- File naming: `YYYY-MM-DD_[Customer]_[Meeting Type].md` or equivalent

### Red Flags
- Notes filed > 48h after the meeting
- Notes that summarize what the PM said (not what the customer said)
- Notes with no action items
- No notes filed at all for a week

## Document Health Standards

### PLT Jira Health (see morning-brief sub-skill for full rules)
- All 10 required fields populated
- Project Status Note updated within 7 days if TED is within 14 days or past
- No PLT in Red status without a status note explaining the situation

### PRD / Strategy Doc Health
- No PRD should go > 2 weeks without an update if it's in active development
- Any PRD going to leadership review should be reviewed by manager first
- Strategy docs should be reviewed against the Heilmeier catechism (see horizon-pm-process skill)

### Hypothesis Tracker Health
- Hypotheses tracker updated after every customer meeting that tests a hypothesis
- No hypothesis remains "Not Yet Tested" for > 30 days if it's assigned to a PM
- Invalidated hypotheses must be explicitly archived (not just ignored)

## Coaching Framework

### Four Action Types

| Type | When to use |
|---|---|
| **(A) Coaching conversation** | Pattern visible in 1:1 data; PM is capable but needs course correction |
| **(B) Unblock now** | Structural issue preventing PM from doing good work (escalation, resource, decision) |
| **(C) Process reminder** | One-off miss that needs a gentle nudge, not a conversation |
| **(D) Escalate up** | Risk or pattern that rises to area leadership level (affects OKR, customer relationship, or team morale) |

### What NOT to do
- Don't run a team-wide process reminder when the issue is with one PM (creates noise, diffuses accountability)
- Don't escalate (D) without first trying to resolve at manager level
- Don't confuse busyness with output — a PM with 10 meetings/week but no hypothesis updates or notes is not producing signal

## Career Development Standards

Each PM should have:
- A clear hypothesis they're responsible for validating this quarter
- At least one "leadership moment" per quarter: presenting to an exec, leading a customer EBC, or owning a cross-functional initiative
- A quarterly coaching conversation focused on the gap between their current level and next level

Manager's role: create the conditions for these moments, then coach on how to capture them.
